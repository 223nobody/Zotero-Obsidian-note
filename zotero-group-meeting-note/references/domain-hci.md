# Domain Template: HCI / Human-Centered Computing

Use this reference for papers about human-computer interaction, user studies, visualization, interactive systems, human-AI collaboration, accessibility, CSCW, education technology, or design tools.

## What To Identify

- Human context: target users, tasks, environment, constraints, expertise level, accessibility needs, and social setting.
- Core intervention: interface, interaction technique, workflow, visualization, prototype, AI assistant, study design, or design framework.
- Evidence type: controlled experiment, field study, diary study, interview, survey, log analysis, usability test, or qualitative coding.
- Baseline fairness: task difficulty, participant pool, counterbalancing, training time, study length, and evaluator bias.

## Innovation Questions

- Does the paper improve performance, comprehension, trust calibration, creativity, accessibility, collaboration, or user agency?
- Is the contribution a system, design space, empirical finding, method, dataset, or theory?
- What part of the result depends on users, context, novelty effects, or study protocol?
- Does the paper show real behavior change, or mainly user preference and perceived usefulness?

## Figures / Tables / Formulas To Explain

- Interface figure: explain workflow, interaction states, information hierarchy, and user decisions.
- Study design diagram: explain conditions, tasks, counterbalancing, and measured outcomes.
- Result table: compare metrics, statistical significance, effect size, and participant variability.
- Qualitative theme table: explain coding process, representative quotes, and evidence boundaries.
- Visualization figure: explain axes, encodings, user tasks, and possible misinterpretations.
- Model or scoring formula: explain what behavior or perception is quantified.

## Related Work Angles

- Interface technique vs empirical study vs design framework.
- Human-AI collaboration vs automation-only systems.
- Lab study vs field deployment.
- Quantitative performance vs qualitative experience.
- General HCI finding vs domain-specific tool.

## Common Limitations

- Participant sample is small, homogeneous, or not representative of target users.
- Tasks are short, artificial, or do not capture long-term use.
- Novelty effects, learning effects, and researcher influence are not controlled.
- Qualitative coding lacks inter-rater agreement or clear evidence trail.
- Claims about real-world adoption exceed the study setting.
